SUMMARY OF CHANGES This document adds the cyberspace operations mission and removes nuclear operations missions.   1.  Mission.  Provide resilient and affordable space and cyberspace forces and capabilities for the Joint  Force  and  the  Nation.    AFSPC  is  an  AF  Major  Command  (MAJCOM)  and  an  AF Component to United States Strategic Command (USSTRATCOM). 2.  Command.  The AFSPC Commander is: 2.1.  Responsible to the Chief of Staff of the AF for administration, training, and readiness of assigned forces. 2.2.  Commander,  AF  Forces  (COMAFFOR)  to  USSTRATCOM  for  space  and  cyberspace forces,  and  exercises  administrative  control  (ADCON)  and  delegated  command  authority over assigned and attached forces.   2  AFMD5  21 AUGUST 2015 2.2.1.  Exercises  operational  control  (OPCON)  and  specified  ADCON  of  Air  Force Reserve Command (AFRC) space assets assigned to USSTRATCOM. 2.3.  The gaining MAJCOM commander for assigned Air National Guard forces, responsible for mobilizing/recalling these forces to active duty, as authorized by Headquarters Air Force (HAF). 2.4.  Responsible  for  identifying  requirements  to  HAF  that  require  mobilization  of  AFRC assets. 3.  Responsibilities.  The Commander, AFSPC will: 3.1.  Organize, train and equip assigned AF space and cyberspace forces. 3.2.  Present integrated space and cyberspace capabilities to Unified Combatant Commands. 3.2.1.  Present USSTRATCOM. space  and  cyberspace  operational forces  and  capabilities to 3.2.2.  Present Command and Control (C2) capabilities to US Northern Command. 3.2.3.  Present  space  and  cyberspace  battlespace  awareness/technical capabilities to USSTRATCOM and Geographic Combatant Commands, as required. intelligence 3.3.  Perform space lift operations to deliver satellites, payloads, and materials into space. 3.4.  Maintain organizational elements to execute the DoD Space Test Program. 3.5.  Serve  as  the  AF  Space  Superiority  and  Cyberspace  Superiority  core  function  leads. Integrate  space  and  cyberspace  capabilities  and  requirements  from  the  HAF,  MAJCOMs, Field Operating Agencies, and Direct Reporting Units. 3.6.  Organize, train and equip forces to support the Service Acquisition Executive, Program Executive Officers, Program Managers, and the Operationally Responsive Space Office with the  acquisition  and  life  cycle  management  planning  of  AF  space-related  systems  and capabilities.  Perform/direct developmental and operational tests of AF space-related systems and capabilities. 3.7.  Support acquisition, sustainment and testing of cyberspace/information technology (IT) capabilities through the AF Lifecycle Management Center under AF Material Command, the responsible agency for design, development, acquisition, sustainment and test. 3.8.  Serve  as  the  lead  MAJCOM  for  space  and  cyberspace  Security  Cooperation  (SC) matters. Support DoD-led international engagements and implement SC activities per signed agreements. 3.9.  Execute  service  specific  cyberspace  actions  for,  and  provide  overall  C2,  security  and defense of the AF Information Network (AFIN). 3.9.1.  Serve  as  the  lead  MAJCOM  for  management  and  oversight  of  AF  cryptographic enterprise requirements and Communications Security/cryptographic modernization. 3.9.2.  In  coordination  with  SAF/CIO  A6,  develop,  coordinate,  implement  and  enforce network configuration management guidelines and standards. 3.10.  Serve  as  a  member  of  the  AF  Space  Board  to  advise  the  Under  Secretary  of  the  AF regarding development of short- and long-term strategies for space capabilities. AFMD5  21 AUGUST 2015   3  3.11.  Serve  as  the  Space  Professional  Functional  Authority  and  Cyberspace  Professional Functional  Authority  responsible  for  providing  strategic-level  guidance  to  the  functional communities that contribute to AF space and cyberspace capabilities. 3.12.  Participate in the AF IT governance structure and support SAF/CIO A6 in developing, implementing and maintaining policies regarding cyberspace and IT for the AF.  LARRY O.
