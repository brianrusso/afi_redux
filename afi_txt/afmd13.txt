SUMMARY OF CHANGES This  Mission  Directive incorporates changes  from  the dissolution  of Joint Task  Force-National Capital  Region-Medical  and  the  activation  of  the  Defense  Health  Agency  and  the  National Capital Region-Medical Directorate (NCR-MD). 1.  Mission. 1.1.  AFDW  provides  Air  Force  Service  component  support  to  Joint  Forces  Headquarters-National  Capital  Region  (JFHQ-NCR)  for  contingency  response,  National  Special  Security Events  (NSSEs),  and  Special  Event  Assessment  Ratings  (SEARs).    Additionally,  AFDW provides  ceremonial  honors  and  operational  support  to  Air  Force  personnel  across  the nationâ€™s capital and worldwide. 2.  Command. 2 AFMD13  25 OCTOBER 2016 2.1.  AFDW is a Direct Reporting Unit to the Chief of Staff of the Air Force (CSAF) and is responsible for administration, training, and readiness of assigned forces. 2.2.  AFDW provides Air Force Service component support for planning and coordination to JFHQ-NCR and is the Air Force supporting command to NCR-MD. 2.3.  AFDW  prepares  and,  when  directed,  transitions  elements  of  AFDW  to  320th  Air Expeditionary  Wing  (320  AEW),  the  Air  Force  Service  component  to  Joint  Task  Force-National Capital Region (JTF-NCR) and provides contingency support. 3.  Responsibilities.  The Commander, AFDW shall: 3.1.  Respond to the CSAF for Air Force administrative and support functions. 3.2.  Serve as the Air Force Service component commander to JFHQ-NCR as assigned by the CSAF by memorandum dated 25 April 2016. 3.3.  Be  prepared  to  serve  as  Commander  Air  Force  Forces  Forward  (COMAFFOR-FWD) for  Air  Force  Forces  (AFFOR)  assigned  and/or  attached  to  the  JTF-NCR.    As  the COMAFFOR-FWD,  serve  as  Senior  Airfield  Authority  for  Joint  Base  Andrews-Naval  Air Facility Washington (JBA-NAFW). 3.4.  Serve as Commander, 320 AEW, upon activation and exercise command and control of AFFOR assigned and attached to 320 AEW. 3.5.  Provide  comprehensive  communications  and  information  management  services  to supported  USAF  organizations  and  activities  within  a  300-mile  radius  of  the  Pentagon  and supported non-USAF tenants on Joint Base Andrews and Joint Base Anacostia-Bolling. 3.6.  Provide medical support to Presidential, Congressional, Joint Staff, and Air Staff special airlift  missions  as  well  as  NCR-MD  and/or  JTF-NCR  as  directed  in  Higher  Headquarters orders. 3.7.  Exercise general, special, and summary court-martial convening authority; authority for actions  under  Article  15,  UCMJ;  administrative  actions;  and  other  actions  requiring command authority over assigned Air Force personnel and designated personnel in the NCR and  worldwide  in  accordance  with  AFPD  51-6,  Command  and  Administrative  Procedures, para. 3.10. 3.8.  Plan  and  coordinate  Air  Force  support  in  the  NCR  for  Homeland  Operations,  Civil Support, NSSEs, SEARs, and designated special/ceremonial events. 3.9.  Execute  Service  administrative  responsibilities  and  support  HAF,  assigned  Air  Force units, designated forward operating  areas, DoD  agencies, US  federal  executive departments and other assigned and attached Air Force personnel within the NCR and worldwide. 3.10.  Provide  combat  ready  forces  in  support  of  Combatant  Commanders  and  Presidential directives. STEPHEN W. WILSON General, USAF Vice Chief of Staff
