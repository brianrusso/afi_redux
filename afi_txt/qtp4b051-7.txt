Notes: *For this QTP the assumption has been made that if an air sampling strategy is needed then there is a need for further (specialized) assessment, thus a less than desirable level of confidence in hazard characterization exists. To identify a course of action (strategy), you must first identify the gap in data that is causing the lack of confidence. A good sampling strategy makes use of both worst-case and typical sampling methods, each selected to answer the questions what, where, when, how, and whom to sample.  **Hazard characterization task steps are outlined in TRAINING MODULE (4.2.2), which is listed as a prerequisite to this QTP.
