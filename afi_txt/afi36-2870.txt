SUMMARY OF CHANGES This  interim  change  revises  AFI36-2870  by  (1)  changing  the  suspense  date  for  award nominations to 15 February (from 31 March), (2) adding the possibility of senior AF civilians to serve  on  the  Nuclear  and  Missile  Operations  awards  board  and  (3)  specifying  any  AFSC  or civilian occupational series within the nuclear enterprise is eligible for the Nuclear Deterrence Operations Award. (4) The interim change creates three distinct categories for the General Larry D. Welch Award (enlisted, officer and civilian). (5) By creating three separate award categories, the  number  of  nominees  each  MAJCOM,  CCMD,  OSD,  Joint  Staff,  HQ  USAF  Staff,  FOA, DRU, etc. may submit for the General Larry D. Welch Award is increased from one to three (one for each category). (6) The interim change deletes the paragraph pertaining to the General Curtis E. Introduction.  This chapter provides procedures for nominating individuals for the Nuclear Deterrence  Operations  (NDO)  Awards.    These  awards  recognize  individuals  and  small  teams from  any  AFSC  or  civilian  occupational  series  within  the  nuclear  enterprise  for  outstanding contributions  to  nuclear  deterrence  operations.    The  USAF  NDO  Core  Function  Master  Plan identifies the following three mission areas within NDO: 3.1.1.  Assure/Dissuade/Deter:  units within this mission area maintain and present credible nuclear capabilities that assure U.S. allies and partners and deter nuclear attack on the U.S., its allies and their forces. 3.1.2.  Nuclear  Strike:    units  within  this  mission  area  maintain  the  ability  to  rapidly  and devastatingly strike targets that an adversary holds dear. 3.1.3.  Nuclear  Surety:    units  within  this  mission  area  ensure  the  safety,  security  and reliability  of  nuclear  weapons  through  a  comprehensive  program  applied  to  materiel, personnel and procedures. 3.2.  Purpose  of  Awards.  The  NDO  awards  are  presented  annually  to  honor  Airmen  who contribute to nuclear deterrence operations. 3.3.  Awards Eligibility.  Regular Air Force, Air National Guard, Air Force Reserve Airmen and Air Force civilians assigned to a unit supporting nuclear deterrence operations according to the eligibility criteria listed in Table 3.1. are eligible for the NDO awards. WELCH AWARD 4.1.  Purpose of the Award  .  This award recognizes the accomplishments of USAF members who,  through  a  single  accomplishment  or  periods  of  accomplishment,  have  most  significantly impacted the overall operations, safety, security, reliability, or effectiveness of the AF nuclear enterprise.  The nuclear enterprise is defined as the people, organizations, processes, procedures and systems that are used to conduct, execute and support nuclear operations and forces.  The intent  is  to  recognize  members  who  have  personally  and  profoundly  impacted  the  nuclear enterprise through an action or accomplishment that has a broad impact above the wing level or across multiple units and/or functional areas. The award is named in honor of General Larry D. Welch, former Chief of Staff of the Air Force, who was a driving force in the reinvigoration of the AF nuclear enterprise.
