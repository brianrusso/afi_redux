Mission.  The  Air  Force  Medical  Support  Agency  (AFMSA),  as  documented  by  paragraph 2.3 of Headquarters Air Force (HAF) Mission Directive 1-48, The Air Force Surgeon General, and  this  Air  Force  Mission  Directive,  directly  supports  the  office  of  the  Air  Force  Surgeon General (AF/SG).  AFMSA provides the expert staff and processes to ensure success of the Air Force  Medical  Service.    The  AFMSA  Commander  asserts  command  authority  over  personnel assigned to  perform  force management, health care operations, research and acquisitions, plans and programming, and financial management. 2.  Organizational and Command Structure. 2.1.  The AFMSA is a field operating agency (FOA) reporting to the AF/SG. 2.2.  The AFMSA Commander: 2 AFMD36  3 OCTOBER 2016 2.2.1.  Is responsible to the Chief of Staff of the Air Force for administration, training and readiness of assigned forces.  Reports to the Deputy Air Force Surgeon General, Office of the AF/SG. 2.2.2.  Provides  support  to  Headquarters  Air  Force  (HAF),  Department  of  Defense (DoD), Major Commands (MAJCOMs), Direct Reporting Units (DRUs) and FOAs, and AF medical treatment facilities (MTFs) on behalf of AFMS interests. 2.2.3.  Partners  with  Assistant  Secretary  of  Defense  for  Health  Affairs  (ASD[HA]), Secretary  of  the  Air  Force,  Chief  of  Staff  of  the  Air  Force,  Department  of  Veterans Affairs,  Defense  Health  Agency  (DHA),  and  numerous  professional  societies  and associations,  civil  organizations,  education,  industry,  press,  and  non-governmental organizations. 2.2.4.  Commands all personnel assigned to AFMSA. Specifically, asserts administrative control  for  personnel  primarily  aligned  under  five  Directorates:    Force  Management (SG1),  Health  Care  Operations  (SG3),  Research  and  Acquisitions  (SG5),  Plans  and Programming (SG8), and Financial Management (SGY). 3.  Responsibilities.  The AFMSA Commander: 3.1.  Executes  efforts  in  support  of  the  strategic  charter  of  the  Air  Force  Medical  Service (AFMS). 3.2.  Exercises direction, guidance, and technical  management of  entire  AFMS to capitalize on the opportunities to improve processes at all levels. 3.3.  Develops  policy,  plans,  resources,  and  decisions  impacting  AFMSA  operational readiness. 3.4.  Works  in  close  coordination  with  MAJCOMs  and  other  health  agencies,  federal  and civilian, to effect maximum utilization of the nation’s medical resources and services. 3.5.  Directs input into the Planning Programming Budgeting and Execution (PPBE) process, such that a balanced Program Objective Memorandum (POM) is developed to meet needs. 3.6.  Works  seamlessly  with  AF/SG  staff  to  accomplish  the  AFMS  mission  to  provide  Air Force medical expeditionary capabilities to support the national security strategy by defining and  executing  health  care  policy  for  44,000  personnel  at  76  MTFs  with  a  $6B  budget; ensuring a  cost-effective, patient-centered, and prevention-based health care continuum  for 2M beneficiaries worldwide; and building strategic partnerships.
