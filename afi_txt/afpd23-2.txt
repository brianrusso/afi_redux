Cooper) Pages: 3    This  directive  implements  guidance  prescribed  in  Department  of  Defense  Instruction  (DoDI) 4140.25, DoD Management Policy for Energy Commodities and Related Services, and Defense Logistics  Agency  (DLA)  Energy  interim  policies  and  procedural  guidance.    Additionally,  this directive  provides  policy  on  managing  and  storing  petroleum  products,  propellants,  chemicals, pure  gases,  and  cryogenic  fluids,  quality  assurance  and  quality  surveillance  and  associated facilities.    This  directive  applies  to  all  military  and  civilian  personnel,  including  major commands (MAJCOMs), Air Force Reserve, Air National  Guard (ANG), and other individuals or organizations as required by binding agreement, contract or obligation with the Department of the  Air  Force,  and  Civil  Air  Patrol  who  receive,  store,  transfer,  issue,  conduct  quality control/quality  surveillance,  and/or  account  for  energy  commodities  on  U.S.  Air  Force installations. The  major changes include Service responsibilities outlines in DoDI 4140.25, which include the Air Force requirement to provide and maintain the capability to deliver bulk petroleum products by air to the Joint Force.  Moreover, it aligns the directive’s formatting with requirements outlined in AFI 33-360, Publications and Forms Management. 1.  Overview. 1.1.  Air  Force  petroleum  products  and  related  materials  are  required  by  the  Air  Force  to properly support its weapon systems, facilities and personnel. 1.2.  The Air  Force must effectively manage  resources  from  the time they are ordered until they are released for use. 1.3.  This directive provides policies for ordering, receiving, transferring, storing, issuing and recovering petroleum products and related materials. 2.  Policy. 2.1.  The Air Force will ensure compliance with Air Force Occupational Safety Instructions, environmental  policies  and  associated  technical  data  for  ordering,  storing,  transferring  and issuing petroleum products and related materials. 2.2.  The  Air  Force  will  take  reasonable  measures  to  ensure  that  stored  petroleum  products and related materials do not harm the environment and are properly recovered. 2.3.  The  Air  Force  will  ensure  inventories  are  consistent  with  peacetime  and  contingency requirements. 2.4.  The  Air  Force  will  establish  programs  for  storing,  accounting  and  handling  petroleum products  and  related  materials  and  ensure  they  are  maintained  in  a  ready  status  to  mitigate deterioration and/or loss of material. 2.5.  The Air Force will maintain the capability to deliver bulk petroleum products by air for those  situations  demanding  immediate  and  emergency  support  to  austere  or  contested locations. 3.  Roles and Responsibilities. 3.1.  The  Deputy  Chief  of  Staff  of  the  Air  Force  for  Logistics,  Engineering  and  Force Protection  (AF/A4)  is  responsible  for  providing  policy,  training,  equipment  and  fuels management guidance for energy commodities handled by the Air Force. 3.2.  Air  Force  commanders/directors  will  ensure  that  all  Air  Force  controlled  petroleum supplies  and  related  materiel  are  received,  stored,  transferred,  issued  and  accurately accounted for and reported to appropriate Air Force and Department of Defense agencies, as required.
