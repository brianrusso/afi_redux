Carlisle) Pages: 2 This  directive  states  the  mission,  defines  the  command  structure,  and  establishes  the responsibilities of Pacific Air Forces (PACAF).  SUMMARY OF CHANGES This  revision  updates  AFMD  8  to  incorporate  format  changes  specified  in  AFI  33-360, Publications  and  Forms  Management.    Mission  and  Responsibilities  are  broadened  to  include cyberspace forces and capabilities.  Responsibilities section is expanded to include Alaska. 1. Mission.  Pacific  Air  Forces  (PACAF)  organizes,  trains,  and  equips  forces  for  independent,joint,  or  combined  military  operations.    PACAF  provides  the  combatant  commander  rapidly deployable,  capabilities-focused  expeditionary  air,  space,  and  cyberspace  forces  to  conduct  the full  spectrum  of  military  operations.    PACAF  also  enhances  transpacific  security,  promotes regional stability, and advances U.S. interests in the Asia/Pacific region.  This organization is a Major Command of the United States Air Force and the Air Force component of United States Pacific Command (USPACOM). 2. Command.  The Commander, PACAF:2.1.  Is  responsible  to  the  Chief  of  Staff  of  the  Air  Force  (CSAF)  for  administration,organizing, training, and equipping of assigned forces.2.2.  Commands Air Force forces assigned/attached to USPACOM.  2  AFMD8  9 April 2012 2.2.1.  By  direction  of  CSAF,  exercises  administrative  control  (ADCON)  over  assigned and attached forces.  ADCON of PACAF-gained Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC) and Air National Guard (ANG) forces is as specified by HQ USAF. 2.2.2.  By direction of Commander, USPACOM, exercises operational control (OPCON) of assigned and attached forces. 3.  Responsibilities.  The Commander, PACAF: 3.1.  Plans,  coordinates,  supports,  and  conducts  employment  of  air,  space,  and  cyberspace operations supporting USPACOM Commander tasks and objectives. 3.2.  On order, provides assigned forces to other combatant commands. 3.3.  Oversees training and readiness of PACAF-gained AFRC and ANG forces. 3.4.  Establishes,  sustains,  improves,  maintains,  and  operates  PACAF  bases  and  facilities throughout the USPACOM AOR and Alaska.  PHILIP M. BREEDLOVE, General, USAF Vice Chief of Staff
