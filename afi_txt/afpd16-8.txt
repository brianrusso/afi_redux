Gaining (supported) commands will direct by contingency plan or tasking message the weapons andarming mission requirements for deploying personnel.9. Terms Explained:9.1. Aircrew.  The total complement of rated (pilots, navigators, and observers) and nonrated person-nel (11XXX Air Force Specialty Code) responsible for the safe ground and flight operation of the air-craft and onboard systems.  Individuals  must be on aeronautical orders and assigned to an authorizedaircrew position.9.2. Personal Defense (PD).  An individual armed for self-defense or to defend someone else.  A per-son is armed for PD when there is a reasonable expectation the armed individual will be isolated fromfriendly forces (for example, aircrews participating in a combat sortie), or when there is a threatagainst the person to be armed or protected.9.3. Selective Arming.  The arming of preselected personnel by installation commanders to defendAir Force resources.  These personnel have access to weapons while performing their primary war-time duty and provide a force multiplier to dedicated base defense forces.9.4. Small Arms.  Hand portable, individual, and crew-served ground weapon systems used mainlyagainst personnel and lightly armored or unarmored equipment.  Officers normally are equipped witha handgun and enlisted personnel with M-16 rifles unless functional, mission, or local requirementsdictate otherwise.9.5. Small Arms Training.  Weapons training programs designed to provide the skills needed byindividuals to handle small arms safely and effectively.10. SeeAttachment 1 for measures used to comply with this policy.11.
